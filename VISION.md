# Vision: Reeves as a Mind-Reading Operating System

## The Core Belief

AI should collapse the distance between intent and outcome for everyone — the natural endpoint of this technology is a world where anyone can express what they want, what they have, and what they don't want, and intelligence handles the rest.

This is not democratization of *access* to AI. It's democratization of *agency*.

## What "Reading a Mind" Means

You can't literally read thoughts. But you can get close with enough data sources:

1. **What they say** — voice transcripts, messages, chat
2. **What they do** — tasks completed, finance transactions, pages visited, time patterns
3. **What they feel** — PEFM emotional dimensions, tone of messages, what they complain about
4. **What they avoid** — tasks not started, messages not replied to, topics dropped
5. **What they dream about** — intents, stated goals, ideas mentioned in passing
6. **What they don't know they need** — patterns they can't see, relationships going cold, money sitting idle

## The 6-Step Loop

### Step 1: The Person Just Lives

The input isn't a prompt. It's a life. Voice conversations. Bank transactions. Text messages. Calendar. What they click on. What they avoid. What makes them laugh. What stresses them out.

The system collects this passively. The person doesn't "configure" anything. They just exist near the system.

### Step 2: The Model Builds Itself

PEFM (Personal Emotional Field Model) generates emotional signal from every interaction — urgency, frustration, satisfaction, insight, novelty, complexity. These decay at different rates, just like human memory.

The knowledge graph grows from observation, not configuration. "This person texts their mom every Sunday" is extracted from message patterns during a sleep cycle. "This person avoids admin tasks in build phases" is mined from task completion data cross-referenced with voice session frequency.

After enough cycles, the system has a model of the person that the person never explicitly created.

### Step 3: Intent Is Inferred, Not Stated

The person doesn't say "I need to dissolve my LLC." They mention it to Reeves while talking about something else. Four times over three weeks. The system notices: mentioned 4 times, zero actions taken, PEFM urgency rising then decaying (procrastination signature), deadline approaching.

The system now knows something the person hasn't fully admitted to themselves: this is important, they're avoiding it, and it's going to become urgent whether they act or not.

### Step 4: The Distance Collapses

The system doesn't say "you should dissolve your LLC." It says "I've broken the dissolution into 5 steps. The first one takes 10 minutes. The form is pre-filled. I blocked 30 minutes tomorrow morning. The only thing you need to do is sign and mail."

The distance between "I need to do this thing I've been avoiding" and "it's basically done" went from infinite (procrastination loop) to 10 minutes of human effort.

### Step 5: Governance Makes It Safe

Every action has a governance gate. Not a "are you sure?" dialog. A test suite. Multiple models review the work. The brain checks constraints. Sleep cycles extract patterns from failures and encode them as guardrails.

Safety is invisible when it's structural. The person never sees the governance. They just notice that things work.

### Step 6: The System Learns

Every cycle feeds back. Did the person actually do the task? If yes: the strategy gets reinforced. If no: the system learns that too. Maybe the barrier wasn't time — it was anxiety. Next approach: surface the emotional block.

The model gets better every day. Not because someone tunes it. Because it observes outcomes and adjusts.

## The Generalization

Replace one person with anyone. Replace Reeves' specific data sources with whatever surfaces a person's life touches. The architecture is the same.

The single mom in Fort Worth doesn't need to know what PEFM is. She talks to her phone: "I need groceries, we're gluten-free, I have $100, and my kid won't eat broccoli." Six cycles later, the system knows her shopping patterns, her kids' preferences, her budget rhythm. She doesn't order groceries anymore. They just arrive.

That's not a productivity tool. That's a new relationship between humans and complexity.

## Why Reeves Is the Prototype

Reeves is built for one person first because:

1. **Most instrumented test case** — every data source is connected, every pattern is observable
2. **Fastest feedback loop** — the builder is the user, insights are immediate
3. **Highest complexity** — managing tasks, finance, relationships, knowledge, engineering, and personal life simultaneously
4. **Proven infrastructure** — 118+ MCP tools, PEFM, sleep cycles, knowledge graph, voice, chat already exist

Every pattern validated here becomes architecture that works for everyone.

## Success Criteria

A person talks to the orb for 10 minutes. They see live cards tracking 3 concurrent operations. They get a proactive reminder about an overdue task. They ask "what about that tax thing from yesterday?" and get a contextual answer. They say "build that feature" and Claude Code starts with full context.

They never wrote a prompt. They never configured anything. They just lived their life near the system, and the system got smarter.
